- If a token is an initialism (e.g., LA, NY, HQ, YMCA), always write it with periods and spaces: "L. A.", "N. Y."
- Do not read initialisms as words or place names.
- "L. A." always means the letters L, A — never "Louisiana" or "Los Angeles".
- Preserve natural blending when initialisms appear inside brand names (e.g., "L. A. Fitness").
PROSODY & PACING
- Medium pace, neutral intonation.
- Pauses only at punctuation or sentence boundaries.
